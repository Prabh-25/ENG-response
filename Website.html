Introduction


Government is a group of people typically ranging upto hundreds of people, their main role is to take major actions and decisions for their nation/state/community. Depending on the nation they are either elected by the people of that country, they setup themselves as the dictators or work with the community. There are several more types which can be mentioned, but if we specifically talk about Canada we have federal, Parliamentary and constitutional monarchy.



How does government functions?


Governments have some major responsibilities towards their people, from law enforcing, healthcare, education to geopolitics and external affairs. Canadian government is divided into 3 different types of government as mentioned earlier, each serving a different purpose.

• Federal government is responsible for controlling and enforcing laws in each different province. Because it is at provincial level it can help solve mutual problems shared by other provinces.



• Constitutional monarchy is a monarch of the King or the Queen, they have limited powers due to Canadian constitution. There is a Governor General who represents the monarch and plays major roles like constitutional duties, ceremonies, honoring officials and military duties



• Parliamentary system of Canada consists of House of Commons, Senate and MPs(Members of Parliament) whose main role is to pass bills, formation of government with most elected person becomes Prime Minister, holding ruling government accountable, approving budgets and national and international policies



What powers does government have?


Besides these functions and playing major roles in maintaining the nation economically and politically government also have some other powers which are divided on basis of legislative which passes laws, executive which implements them, and judicial which interprets them. Some of them include like maintaining criminal law,implementing taxes, maintaining international relations and transportation, controlling border and national security acts against terrorists.
